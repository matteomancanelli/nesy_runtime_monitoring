\section{Theoretical Comparison}
\label{sec:theory-cmp}

Having presented the three paradigms individually, we now compare them along the dimensions that matter for a monitor: what each can represent, and where each breaks down.

\subsection{Three Achilles Heels}
\label{sec:theory-heels}

Each paradigm is exact and efficient on a large class of specifications, and each has a single structural weakness that is dual to the others', so that no one paradigm dominates.
%
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Symbolic DFA --- state-space blow-up.} The \ltlf-to-DFA translation is doubly exponential in the worst case, so the compiled automaton (and hence the compile-time cost and memory) can explode even when the runtime visits only a handful of states.
    \item \textbf{RuleRunner --- nested-temporal conflation.} The one-register-per-subformula state cannot represent disjunctions of concurrently-live obligations, so it returns wrong verdicts on temporal-under-temporal nesting (Section~\ref{sec:rulerunner-limitation}); restoring completeness forces the state up to DFA size (Section~\ref{sec:rulerunner-progression}).
    \item \textbf{DeepDFA --- alphabet blow-up.} The transition tensor is indexed by $2^\calP$, and guards depending on all atoms admit no sub-exponential symbol set to sum over unless their read-once circuit structure is exposed (Section~\ref{sec:deepdfa-alphabet}).
\end{itemize}
%
Two clarifications keep this comparison honest. First, the state-space blow-up is not exclusive to the symbolic paradigm: DeepDFA is compiled from the \emph{same} minimal DFA, so it inherits $|Q|$ (its tensor is $|Q|^2 \cdot 2^{|\calP|}$) and suffers the same doubly-exponential worst case. We list state blow-up under the symbolic monitor because it is that monitor's \emph{only} structural weakness, whereas for DeepDFA the \emph{additional}, representation-specific cost that the symbolic monitor escapes is the alphabet axis. Second, the two neuro-symbolic-versus-symbolic heels are duals in a precise sense: a DFA transition function is a table with $|Q| \cdot 2^{|\calP|}$ entries, and each paradigm compresses a different axis of it to obtain its advantage, paying on the axis it leaves explicit. The symbolic monitor compresses the $2^\calP$ axis with symbolic guards --- keeping an explicit current state, so its cost driver is $|Q|$ --- and this is exactly why it cannot suffer an alphabet blow-up: it never enumerates symbols. DeepDFA (dense) compresses the state axis into a uniform, differentiable, batchable matrix product --- which requires one explicit transition matrix per symbol, so its cost driver is $2^{|\calP|}$. Neither weakness can be swapped onto the other paradigm without discarding the very representational choice that defines it; the factored representation (Section~\ref{sec:deepdfa-factored}) is precisely the attempt to compress \emph{both} axes at once, and recovers the flat-in-$|\calP|$ scaling on the read-once guards where such a compression exists.

As monitors of crisp traces, the completeness-restored RuleRunner and DeepDFA are interchangeable --- the progression construction converges to the same minimal DFA (Section~\ref{sec:rulerunner-progression}), so on any crisp trace they agree. The reason to keep both lineages is what each exposes beyond crisp monitoring: DeepDFA parameterizes an opaque transition tensor, while the rule-based construction attaches its parameters to syntactically-indexed rules --- two different substrates for the gradient-based specification adaptation we defer to future work (Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}).
